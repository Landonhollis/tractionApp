# Font Directory for AI Selection

## Selection Biases for AI Agents
1. **Prefer variable fonts** (adjustable weights) over static fonts for flexibility
2. **Prioritize popular, well-established fonts** for proven reliability
3. **Maintain consistency** by selecting fonts already used in the app when appropriate
4. **Choose readable fonts** for body text, paragraphs, and lengthy content
5. **Reserve not-readable fonts** for headlines, accents, and short text only

---

## READABLE FONTS (Suitable for Longer Text)

### **Tinos** (Variable: Regular, Italic, Bold, Bold Italic) P
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Professional, familiar, trustworthy, traditional
**Use for:** Academic papers, reports, documents, resumes, body text, formal content. Metrically compatible with Times New Roman - use when replacing TNR or need reliable serif readability. Excellent screen readability.
**Bias:** Highly recommended - variable font with proven track record.

### **DM Serif Text** (Regular, Italic) P
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** No
**Feel:** Elegant yet approachable, balanced, modern-traditional blend
**Use for:** Body text in books, magazines, articles, blog posts, website content. Perfect for narrative text and longer passages where sophisticated readability matters.
**Bias:** Excellent for text applications, though static weights limit flexibility.

### **Merriweather Sans** (Variable + Italic) P
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Friendly, professional, warm, clear
**Use for:** UI text, website body copy, digital interfaces, long-form content, mobile applications. Designed specifically for screen readability at small sizes.
**Bias:** Highly recommended - variable, humanist design excels at extended reading.

### **Instrument Sans** (Variable + Italic) P
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Clean, modern, balanced, playful precision
**Use for:** UI/UX design, app interfaces, professional branding, body text, general content. Versatile geometric sans with 12 stylistic sets for customization.
**Bias:** Top choice - variable, neutral, highly versatile for modern applications.

### **Josefin Sans** (Variable + Italic)
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Elegant, geometric, vintage-modern, airy
**Use for:** Headlines, titles, large text elements, branding. Works for body text but best at larger sizes due to unusual x-height proportions (halfway to cap height). Scandinavian-inspired 1920s-30s aesthetic.
**Bias:** Good for headers but use cautiously for body text. Variable font advantage.

### **Markazi Text** (Variable)
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Clear, flowing, professional, cultured
**Use for:** Arabic, Persian, Urdu content. E-books, articles, multilingual interfaces. Best Arabic web font for extended reading with excellent support for both Arabic and Latin scripts.
**Bias:** Essential if app needs Arabic/Persian support. Variable font.

### **Radley** (Regular, Italic)
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** No
**Feel:** Classic, sophisticated, hand-crafted warmth, charming
**Use for:** Editorial design, book covers, narrative text, formal invitations, branding requiring elegance. Hand-carved letterforms give unique character while maintaining readability.
**Bias:** Use when character matters, but limited weights reduce versatility.

### **Playfair Display** (Variable + Italic) ⭐
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Luxurious, sophisticated, elegant, high-fashion
**Use for:** Editorial content, magazine articles, fashion copy, luxury branding, upmarket publications, headers. High contrast transitional serif that works beautifully for both headlines and sophisticated body text in editorial contexts. Popular in fashion and lifestyle publishing.
**Bias:** Highly recommended for luxury/fashion/editorial - variable font, widely recognized, very readable despite elegant styling.

### **Bodoni Moda** (Variable + Italic) ⭐
**Type:** Readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Sophisticated, classic, refined, luxurious
**Use for:** Editorial body text, magazine copy, luxury branding, upmarket publications, high-gloss print. Modern serif with high contrast suitable for both headlines and text. Full range of weights makes it versatile for editorial design.
**Bias:** Excellent for high-end editorial and branding - variable font, timeless appeal, readable at various sizes.

## NOT-READABLE FONTS (Display, Decorative, Short Text Only)

### **Orbitron** (Variable)
**Type:** Not-readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Futuristic, technical, modern, sci-fi, energetic
**Use for:** Tech branding, gaming, sci-fi projects, headlines, logos, posters. Square geometric forms evoke technology and innovation. Perfect for forward-thinking brands.
**Bias:** Best choice for tech/sci-fi aesthetic - variable font flexibility.

### **Caveat** (Variable)
**Type:** Not-readable | **Adjustable:** Yes
**Feel:** Casual, personal, friendly, informal, warm
**Use for:** Short annotations, captions, greeting cards, invitations, personal notes, social media graphics. Natural handwriting feel but informal nature limits professional use.
**Bias:** Good for personal touch - variable weights useful, but keep usage minimal.

### **Alfa Slab One** (Regular)
**Type:** Not-readable | **Adjustable:** No
**Feel:** Bold, striking, impactful, contemporary, attention-grabbing
**Use for:** Headlines, posters, logos, labels, book titles. Extreme weight and large serifs demand attention. 1920s-inspired with modern edge.
**Bias:** Powerful for headlines but single weight limits versatility.

### **Diplomata SC** (Regular)
**Type:** Not-readable | **Adjustable:** No
**Feel:** Formal, luxurious, sophisticated, classic elegance
**Use for:** Luxury branding, formal invitations, editorial headlines, high-end packaging, certificates. Bold decorative serif with high contrast suggests prestige.
**Bias:** Use sparingly for premium contexts only.

## Summary for AI Selection

**For body text and paragraphs:** Use Tinos, DM Serif Text, Merriweather Sans, Instrument Sans, Playfair Display, or Bodoni Moda (prefer variable fonts: Tinos, Merriweather Sans, Instrument Sans, Playfair Display, Bodoni Moda).

**For editorial/magazine content:** Playfair Display and Bodoni Moda are excellent - both readable and elegant.

**For UI elements and interfaces:** Use Instrument Sans, Merriweather Sans, or Josefin Sans.

**For elegant content (readable):** Use Playfair Display, Bodoni Moda, or Radley.

**For tech/sci-fi display:** Use Orbitron (variable) - not-readable, short text only.

**For personal touch:** Use Caveat (variable) sparingly - not-readable.

**For formal elegance (not-readable):** Use Ephesis or Whisper in very small amounts.

**Variable fonts to strongly prefer:** Tinos, Instrument Sans, Merriweather Sans, Playfair Display, Bodoni Moda, Josefin Sans, Markazi Text (all readable); Orbitron, Caveat, Sixtyfour (not-readable).

**Default recommendation:** Start with Instrument Sans for UI/body and Playfair Display for editorial/headlines - both are variable, popular, versatile, and readable.
